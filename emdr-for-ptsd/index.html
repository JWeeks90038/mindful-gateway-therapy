EMDR for PTSD

EMDR is often considered by people with PTSD who want help reducing the emotional charge of traumatic memories and triggers. It can be part of a structured treatment approach that supports more adaptive processing of painful experiences.

EMDR for PTSD works best when there is adequate preparation, emotional safety, and a pace that fits the client’s nervous system.

FAQ

Is EMDR effective for PTSD?

EMDR is widely recognized as an evidence-based treatment for PTSD and trauma-related symptoms.

What if I feel nervous about EMDR?

That is common. A good process includes preparation, questions, and pacing rather than rushing into difficult material.

Learn Whether EMDR Fits Your Needs

PTSD treatment can be individualized. Therapy can help you decide whether EMDR is the right option.
