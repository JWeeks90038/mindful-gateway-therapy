Childhood Trauma in Adults

Childhood trauma can continue to shape adult life through hypervigilance, people-pleasing, self-criticism, trust issues, emotional shutdown, or anxiety that seems hard to explain. Many adults do not immediately connect their current struggles to early experiences because those patterns have simply felt normal for a long time.

Therapy can help uncover how early experiences shaped survival strategies and how those strategies may no longer be serving you now.

FAQ

Can childhood trauma affect anxiety?

Yes. Childhood trauma can contribute to chronic worry, panic, emotional sensitivity, and nervous system overactivation in adulthood.

Can adults heal from childhood trauma?

Yes. Healing is possible through trauma-informed therapy, emotional regulation work, and compassionate understanding of survival patterns.

Support for Early Wounds

If childhood experiences still affect your life today, therapy can help you work toward safety and change.
