When to Seek Help for PTSD

It may be time to seek help for PTSD when symptoms are affecting your sleep, relationships, work, emotional regulation, or sense of safety. Many people wait because they think they should be able to handle it alone, but trauma symptoms often persist until they are directly addressed.

Reaching out does not require certainty. If you suspect trauma may still be affecting you, therapy can help clarify what is happening and what support may help.

FAQ

Do I need a diagnosis before reaching out?

No. Therapy can begin with understanding symptoms and deciding what kind of support fits best.

What if I am functioning but struggling inside?

That still matters. Many people seek help while outwardly functioning well but privately feeling overwhelmed or constantly on edge.

Reach Out Before It Gets Worse

You do not have to wait for a breaking point to begin getting support.
