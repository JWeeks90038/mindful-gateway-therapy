Understanding Bereavement

Bereavement refers to the period of adjustment and mourning after someone dies. It includes grief, but it can also include practical disruptions, identity shifts, family role changes, and the painful task of learning how life continues after a loss.

Bereavement affects people emotionally, physically, socially, and spiritually. Understanding that wide impact can help normalize why loss feels so all-encompassing.

FAQ

Is bereavement the same as grief?

They are related but not identical. Bereavement refers to the condition of loss, while grief often refers to the emotional response to that loss.

How long does bereavement last?

There is no single timeline. Bereavement often changes over time rather than simply ending.

Support During Bereavement

Loss affects more than feelings alone. Therapy can help with the wider impact too.
